Pitch


Oishi is an app for young actives who need to shop groceries and want to reduce their environemental impact according to their constraints. They choose Oishi because it enables them to adapt to their consumer behaviors while being educated to a more conscient way to do the groceries shopping.


Competitors


Eat.ch / UberEat / Leshop.ch / Yuka.io


UX Persona



User Journey


Context


Where : while commuting on her way back to home and uni lecture break

When : 7pm and 10:30am

With who : Alone or with a roommate just behind her

Which device : She is on her smartphone

Which mood : She had a long day studing, tired and anxious for her future


1. Anna goes on the web application

2. She selects the products she wants

3. She looks at shopping lists offered by the app

4. She make a few swaps with the two offered lists to make her perfect list

5. She checks once again dates and price of delivery

6. She orders what its on her list and pay

7. She looks at her Dashboard to check quickly her previous orders and check her rewards, only her can see that

8. She leaves a review on her last order

9. She will fetch her delivery to the closest Hub


Features


Users

As a user I can order products on my shopping list, so that I can order products on my shopping list and be redirected to the payment platform

As a user I can save a shopping list, so that I can save shopping lists for future orders

As a user I can display my consumer dashboard, so that I can display my dashboard to check my consumption details (spendings, average and detailed eco impact, nutritional details, current orders, previous orders)


Visitors

As a visitor I can visit the home page, so that I discover the proposition value and learn how it works

As a visitor I can view a product's page and see recommendations, so that I can check details about a product and check for suggestions. I can also see the eco impact and nutritional properties and price

As a visitor I can add products to my shopping list, so that Ican add a product from the index or the product page on my shopping list and continue to shop

As a visitor I can cancel an element from the list, so that I can remove an element from the shopping list I am making

As a visitor I can update a shopping list element for quantity of one article, so that I can update a shopping list before ordering it

As a visitor I can browse suggestted playlists, so that I can check pre-done shopping lists with recommendations according to my preferences

As a visitor I can display the order overview, so that I can display the cart overview before order and payment

Pitch


Skatrr is a mobile app to locate street spots and upload skate videos about best lines and tricks. Skaters choose Skatrr to get out of traditional crowded skate parks or damaged structures, and find some creative and secret spots.


Context


Skateboarding is everywhere. You see it on TV, in movies, on cereal boxes, the Internet, and around town. Skateboarding is popular with spectators and participants. Skaters and their fans come from all walks of life. They’re men and women, boys and girls. You might be a skateboarder, or you know a skateboarder. If you’re a teenager, the odds are good that you’ve stood on a skateboard within the last year, and you know lots of people that skate. Many adults spent their youth skateboarding. People all over the world skate and there are skateparks to prove it. Even in the smallest towns and villages you can find a few local youth working on their skateboarding skills. It is surprising to learn that many cities and towns don’t have a single skatepark or in poor condition. It’s easy to find a football field almost anywhere you go. Yet when it comes to skateboarding, skaters have little choice but to ride on the sidewalks, on the streets, school campuses, parking lots, and other places around town… or simply don’t skate at all.


Target


Of all skaters, 77.1% are male, whereas 83.4% of core skaters are male. Conversely, 23.9% of all skaters are female, while 16.6% of all core skaters are female.

Skateboarding, for many, becomes the recreational activity of choice well into adulthood. Market research studies reveal that skateboarders are, on average, 14 year old males. 40% of skateboarders are between 6 to 12 years old and 30% are 13 to 17 years old (source: BoardTrac). It also appear that it is the new generation that is the most concerned by using social networks and are confortable with using new digital technologies.

The hallmark of skateboarding culture is that it is welcoming to anyone that approaches it with the intention of improving his or her personal skill. Class, race, gender, weight, and other hobbies are irrelevant provided that the participant’s enthusiasm for the actual act of skateboarding is genuine. In this way, skateboarding is egalitarian and inclusive. Because skateboarding is not a team sport, the success and/or enjoyment of skaters is not dependent on other skaters’ performance. In other words, an experienced skateboarder can recreate with someone learning fundamental skills as equals. There is nothing to gain or lose from seeing other people land a trick for the first time except for the joy of seeing them do it.
